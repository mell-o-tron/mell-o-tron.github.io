 
Łoś's theorem

$$\prod_{i \in I} M_i/U \models \varphi([a_1] ... [a_k]) \iff \{i \in I \mid M_i \models \varphi(a_{1i} ... a_{ki})\} \in U$$

### Notation

Let $vl(\varphi) \subseteq \{v_1 ... v_k\}$, let us denote by $\varphi(v_1 ... v_k)$ the fact that the validity of $\varphi$ depends on $v_1...v_k$, and let us write 
$$M \models \varphi(a_1...a_n)$$
to say that $M \models\{v\}\varphi$ holds provided that $v(v_1) = a_1$, ..., $v(v_k) = a_k$.

### Corollary

- If $\forall i \in I$ holds $M_i \models \varphi_i$, then $\prod_{i \in I} M_i / U \models \varphi$. 

- If $U$ is non-principal, it suffices that $M_i \models \varphi_i$ for all $i$ except a finite number.
